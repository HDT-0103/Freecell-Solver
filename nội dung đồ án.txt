Project 1
FreeCell Solver
1	Introduction
FreeCell  is a popular solitaire game that uses a standard 52-card deck. It became widely known after being included as a free desktop game in early versions of MS Windows operating system. In a typical session, the deck is dealt face up into 8 cascades, with 4 free cells and 4 foundation. The objective is to move all cards to the foundations by suit, in ascending order from Ace to King, using the free cells and cascades strategically.
 
Figure 1: A screenshot of the Microsoft FreeCell game.
This game is quite suitable for practicing search algorithms, such as BFS, DFS, UCS, and A*. First, it is easily accessible, students can quickly grasp the rules and relate them to the problem. Next, unlike many other solitaire games, all cards are face-up from the beginning, with no hidden or any random elements, allowing us to treat the searching as a fully observable planning problem. Finally, defining states, actions, and transitions is straightforward, and admissible heuristics can be constructed, making this an excellent problem for designing and analyzing heuristic search methods like A*.
2	Gameplay
The following Freecell rules are taken from Wikipedia. You can try the game via this link.
2.1	Construction and layout
One standard 52-card deck is used for the game. There are 4 empty cells and 4 empty foundations.
Cards are dealt face-up into 8 cascades (columns), 4 columns of 7 cards and 4 columns of 6 cards.
2.2	Moves
Any cell card or top card of any cascade may be moved to build on a tableau, or moved to an empty cell, an empty cascade, or its foundation. Complete or partial tableaus may be moved to build on existing tableaus, or moved to empty cascades, by recursively placing and removing cards through intermediate locations.
•	Move a single card from the top of a cascade to:
–	An empty free cell.
–	The top of another cascade if it forms a valid descending sequence of alternating colors (e.g., 7♠ on 8♡).
•	Move a card from a cascade or a free cell to a foundation pile if:
–	It matches the suit of that foundation and
–	It is exactly one rank higher than the current top card of that foundation (starting from Ace up to King).
•	Allow moving sequences of cards between cascades as long as:
–	The sequence itself is a valid descending alternating-color sequence, and
–	Its length does not exceed the limit imposed by the number of empty free cells and empty cascades (i.e., the sequence can be thought of as being moved one card at a time under standard FreeCell rules).
2.3	Victory
The game is won after all 52 cards have been moved to the foundations in correct order.
